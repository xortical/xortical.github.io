Debayan Dasgupta

Nanorobotics · Active Matter · Biomedical Micromachines

I work at the intersection of physics, nanotechnology, and biology, building magnetically driven microrobots that can move, sense, and interact with complex biological environments.

My research explores how active systems behave inside cells, tissues, and porous biological structures and how those dynamics can be translated into minimally invasive medical technologies.

What I Do

• Magnetic nanorobots and helical microswimmers
• Active matter and stochastic dynamics
• Rheology of biological and soft materials
• Image-based biophysics and computational analysis
• Translation of deep-tech research into clinical tools

Startup

I am a co-founder of Theranautilus, a deep-tech startup developing nanorobotic systems for oncology treatments and regenerative medicine. Our work focuses on reaching anatomical regions inaccessible to conventional tools and deliver medicaments precisely.
